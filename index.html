My Contribution

My biggest contribution comes from an internship I had with Lockheed Martin last summer as a Software Engineer. Here,
I was placed in a team of nine experienced employees who helped me learn more about the company and life in the workforce.
During this time, I helped the company by cooperating with my teammates in optimizing aircraft software through writing
new features and updating older code. As a result, I gained experience in debugging, utilizing functions with fewer run times,
and testing code to ensure the product works before releasing it. I even gained non-technical skills through teamwork,
Agile planning, and networking with workers outside of my team. The biggest lessons I learned is to have fun at work,
take initiative to learn new skills, and pay close attention to what I'm working on. These lessons are important to me
because I get to know my teammates better and build each other's trust through fun, I learn from first hand experience on how
to get tasks done by taking initiative, and think about the importance of having a trustworthy product for our customers,
in this case our Armed Forces, to use in the field through attention to detail.
Link to Lockheed Martin: www.lockheedmartin.com/en-us/index.html.

Service

Big Event

Ever since freshman year, I've participated in Big Event, which is the largest student-run service project at Texas A&M.
The purpose of Big Event is to help residents in the Bryan/College Station area in various home improvement projects.
So far, I've participated Big Event three times with other cadets, all with different projects:
Freshman Year: cleaned toys and cleared a backyard for a child daycare home.
Sophomore Year: planted mulch on trees in the frontyard and backyard for an elderly resident.
Junior Year: site leader for a project that involved planting trees, cutting old tree roots, and cleaning an attic for a family.
bigevent.tamu.edu.

Toys for Tots

Toys for Tots is a United States Marine Corps Reserve program that donates toys to families who can't afford toys for
their children. In my freshman and sophomore year, my Corps outfit had the opportunity to help Toys for Tots by
standing outside Kyle Field before Midnight Yell and ask atendees if they would like to donate money. The majority
of the donations would go to the organization, while the rest would go into my outfit's funds.
www.toysfortots.org.

Scotty's House

Scotty's House is a non-profit Child Advocacy Center that helps children suffering from child abuse and/or neglect through
assessment, counseling, and education. Last semester, as a Major Unit Public Relations/Recruiting Officer in charge of five
PR/Recruiting chains in five outfits in the Corps, I scheduled a volunteer project with this group that involved creating
an outside walking trail for child therapy sessions. I was able to get about twenty cadets to sign up for this project.
Additionally, my Major Unit directly donates money to Scotty's House to help them with their operations and for the cadets
in the Class of 2021 in my five outfits to compete on which outfit gets to raise the most money to be Officers of the Day
for an Aggie football game for the 2020 season.
www.scottyshouse.org.
